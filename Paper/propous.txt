Project: Multi-Subject Personalization Evaluation (P13N Workshop)

Goal:
Study whether subject-driven diffusion models can compose many identities under interaction and occlusion.

--------------------------------
1. Build Subject Pool
--------------------------------
Source subjects from two personalization datasets:
- XVerse
- COSMISC

Steps:
1. Collect subject reference images.
2. Merge them into a single subject pool.
3. Assign each subject a unique ID (S001, S002, ...).

Example structure:
dataset/
    subjects/
        S001.jpg
        S002.jpg
        ...

--------------------------------
2. Construct Multi-Subject Benchmark
--------------------------------
Create evaluation prompts with increasing subject counts.

Subject levels:
2 subjects
4 subjects
6 subjects
8 subjects
10 subjects

Prompts per level:
10 prompts

Prompt types:
- interaction
- occlusion
- neutral

Examples:
"A hugging B"
"A standing behind B"
"A hugging B while C stands behind D"

Total prompts:
5 levels × 10 prompts = 50 prompts

Save benchmark as:
benchmark.json

--------------------------------
3. Run Models
--------------------------------
Evaluate the following personalization models:

Models:
- XVerse: https://github.com/bytedance/XVerse
- COSMISC: https://github.com/bytedance-fanqie-ai/MOSAIC
- PSR: https://github.com/wang-shulei/PSR

Input:
prompt + subject reference images

Generation setup:
3 random seeds per prompt

Total generations:
50 prompts × 3 seeds × 3 models ≈ 450 images

Save results:
results/
    xverse/
    cosmic/
    psr/

--------------------------------
4. Compute Evaluation Metrics
--------------------------------

Metric 1: CLIP score
Measure text-image alignment.

CLIP(image, prompt)

Metric 2: NIDO
Measure identity preservation.

NIDO(reference_subject, generated_image)

Metric 3: SCR (Subject Collapse Rate)
Measure how many subjects disappear or collapse.

SCR = collapsed_subjects / total_subjects

Collapse rule:
CLIP(reference_subject, generated_image) < τ

Thresholds:
τ = 0.2
τ = 0.25
τ = 0.3

--------------------------------
5. Aggregate Results
--------------------------------

For each subject level compute:

mean CLIP
mean NIDO
mean SCR

Expected trend:

subjects ↑
CLIP ↓
NIDO ↓
SCR ↑

--------------------------------
6. Visualization
--------------------------------

Create main plots:

1) subjects → CLIP
2) subjects → NIDO
3) subjects → SCR

Also show:

- interaction vs non-interaction
- qualitative failure cases

--------------------------------
7. Paper Writing
--------------------------------

Sections:

1 Introduction
2 Related Work
3 Multi-Subject Benchmark
4 Evaluation Metrics
5 Experiments
6 Discussion
7 Conclusion

Key claim:
Subject-driven diffusion models fail to compose many interacting identities.
